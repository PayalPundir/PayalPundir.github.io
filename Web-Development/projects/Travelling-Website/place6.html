Welcome to Rajasthan

Rajasthan, a stunning fusion of colour, grandeur, history, and romance, is a favoured destination for foreign visitors to India. Even seasoned tourists struggle to decide what to do and see in Rajasthan since there are so many alternatives available. Rajputana was ruled by Maharajas a century ago. They were devoted supporters of culture and the arts. Today, there are many magnificent fortifications, glittering palaces, traditional dance, and arts that show all of their eccentricities as well as their appreciation for tradition and art. The annual Pushkar Fair, which takes place in November, is one of the reasons Rajasthan is one of the top places to visit in winter in India. The biggest cattle fair in the world is held at Pushkar Fair.

Places to Visit:

Amer Fort, Udaipur, Jaipur, Jaisalmer, and many more.

How to Reach:
By Air:

The key airport in Rajasthan is the Jaipur International Airport. There are other airports in Rajasthan, including Udaipur Airport, Kota Airport, Jaisalmer Airport, Bikaner Airport and Jodhpur Airport.

By Train:

The key railway stations in Rajasthan are in Udaipur, Jodhpur, Ajmer and Jaipur.

By Road:

Rajasthan boasts of 20 national highways; the most well-linked being the NH8, which connects Rajasthan to Delhi, Udaipur, Mumbai, Vadodara, Ahmedabad, etc.
