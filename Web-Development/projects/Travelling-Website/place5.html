Welcome to Kerala

Kerala’s genuine natural grandeur reaches its peak after the rainy season, yet it is a delight in every season. Kerala’s attraction is enhanced by the Western Ghats and brackish lagoons or backwaters. Backwaters, beaches, spice gardens, tea plantations, Ayurveda, ecotourism, and an abundance of wildlife—you name it, Kerala has it. Kerala is one of the best winter vacation spots in India because of this. Beach enthusiasts should visit Kovalam, Varkala, or even Marari, while those who like backwaters may visit Alappuzha, Kollam, Kumarakom, or the Vembanad Lake. Then there is the vast tea plantation and pleasant climate of Munnar. There’s a bird sanctuary in Kumarakom, a tiger reserve in Periyar, and the world’s most biodiverse environment if you are interested in animals.

Places to Visit:

Virupaksha Temple, Monkey Temple, Lotus Temple, and many more.

How to Reach:
By Air:

Three international airports are located in Kerala. The state’s southernmost region is where the Trivandrum International Airport is situated. The state’s core is where you’ll find the Kochi International Airport. The international airport at Calicut, which is in the state’s north, receives the majority of foreign passengers. In addition to these, Kerala is connected to nearly all of the main states in the nation via a large number of domestic airports. You may take buses, trains, and taxis to specific locations from the airport. Additionally, there are prepaid cabs at the airport.

By Train:

In Kerala, there are over 200 railroad stations. To get to certain locations, you may find a lot of cars and cabs outside the train station.

By Road:

Kerala has three highways that provide good access. Three highways, NH 49, NH 47, and NH 17, link the state. Additionally, it is linked to all neighbouring states by well-built roadways. Private and public buses are available for travel to Kerala from several significant states and cities around the nation. To get to Kerala, you could also rent a taxi from any major city.s
