Welcome to Nainital

The picturesque hill town of Nainital is situated in Uttarakhand at the base of the Kumaon mountains. It is the most travelled hill station in North India and is situated near Dehradun and Delhi. The ideal weekend retreat from Delhi and the surrounding areas is Nainital. Nainital is a well-liked hill resort for families, couples, and even lone travellers because of its lovely year-round temperature. It is convenient for a two-day stay and can be reached by car from adjacent large cities. The bus stop is located just across from Naina Lake, with Mall Road and Thandi Sadak on each side. Nainital, which was established by the British because of its likeness to the Cumbrian Lake District, is filled with exquisite colonial buildings that add to the areaâ€™s natural beauty. Near Nainital, there are a few undiscovered little hill settlements, including Pangot, Ranikhet, and Almora. There are also a few short hiking routes in Nainital.

Places to Visit:

Bhimtal Lake, Naina Peak, Eco Cave Gardens, Kainchi Dham, and many more.

How to Reach:
By Air:

You can board the flight to Pantnagar Airport from any major Indian city, then board the bus or hire a cab to Nainital, which is nearly 70km from the airport. From there, you can either hire a cab or board a bus to Nainital, which is 34 km away.

By Train:

You can take the train to Kathgodam railway station. From there, you can either hire a cab or board a bus to Nainital, which is 34 km away.

By Road:

Nainital can be reached by state or private buses and people can also drive themselves there.
