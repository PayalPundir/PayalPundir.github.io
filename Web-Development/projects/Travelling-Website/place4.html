Welcome to Hampi

Hampi, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, will transport you to a bygone period with its architectural wonders. Despite being a hot location in general, December and January have the greatest weather, rendering it one of the top winter tourist destinations in India. Additionally, it serves as the location for the Hampi Mahotsav in January. One of the greatest destinations to visit in South India during the winter is the festival, which honours Karnataka culture with puppet displays, temple procession, sports, dance, music, and more.

Places to Visit:

Virupaksha Temple, Monkey Temple, Lotus Temple, and many more.

How to Reach:
By Air:

The nearest domestic airport to Hampi is Hubli Airport, which is around 166 kilometres distant and has daily flights from Bangalore. From the airportâ€™s curb, tourists may simply take a taxi or cab to take them to the charming hamlet of Hampi.

By Train:

The closest train station to Hampi is Hospet Junction, which is 13 km distant. Hospet Junction is well connected to the rest of the nation by a vast railroad network.

By Road:

Bus services connect Hampi with the major Karnataka cities and towns. A few statewide bus routes also connect Hampi to several Goan, Andhra Pradesh, and Maharashtrian cities.
