Welcome to Auli

There is no shortage of beautiful nature in Auli, which is scattered with apple orchards, ancient oaks, and pine trees. In addition to skiing, the slopes of the Garhwal Himalayas offer a variety of excursions where you may take in the breathtaking vistas of the snow-covered mountains. In the Himalayan range, Auli is a well-known hill resort that dates back to the eighth century AD. Both a ski resort and a ski rental business are handled by the Garhwal Mandal Vikas Nigam Limited (GMVL). Because of its sparkling slopes and tidy surroundings, Auli is a well-known skiing resort in India. Auli is a well-known hill town with multiple ski resorts set within the Himalayan range, studded with apple orchards, oaks, and deodar trees. The Nanda Devi, Mana Parvat, and Kamat Kamet mountain ranges may be found there, 2800 metres above sea level. Around Auli, there are several places of worship. How to Reach:
By Air:

You can board the flight to the Jolly Grant Airport in Dehradun from any major Indian city. From there, you can board a taxi to Auli, which is 286km away from the airport.

By Train:

You can take the train to Rishikesh Railway Station. From there, you can board a taxi/bus to Auli, which is 264km away from Rishikesh.

By Road:

You can board a bus from ISBT Kashmiri Gate, Delhi, to Rishikesh. You can board a bus or a cab from Rishikesh to Auli. You have to take a cable car from Joshimath to Auli in case of heavy snowfalls.
