Welcome to Varanasi

The nightly arti, a highly choreographed show that takes place at the Dashwamedh Ghat, is currently the most recognisable emblem of Varanasi. Varanasi is renowned for its enthusiasm for secularism. This city is linked to several Indian saints and poets, including Kabir and Tulsidas. However, if you are coming here, heed this warning. The city is chaotic, to say the least, and it spreads out from the Ganges Ghats in a network of streets that are too tiny for traffic and fairly congested. The city, which is regarded as the throbbing heart of Hinduism, might be visited at any time of the year save during the monsoon, but like other places in the North Indian plains, winter is the finest season. Varanasi is one of the greatest locations in India to travel to in January due to the milder climate and lack of humidity.

Places to Visit:

Iskcon temple, Durga Temple, Batuk Bhairav Temple, New Vishwanath Temple, Ganga Aarti, and many more.

How to Reach:
By Air:

You can board the flight to Varanasi or the Lal Bahadur Shastri Airport from any major Indian city.

By Train:

You can take the train to the Varanasi Railway Station or Kashi Railway Station.

By Road:

You can board a bus or hire a taxi or drive yourself to Varanasi since it has a well-connected road network.
